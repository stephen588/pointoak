How do I know if my current 401(k) fees are too high?

Compare your all-in fees to plans of similar size and industry. Research shows plans under $1 million in assets average 1.26% in fees, while plans over $1 billion average just 0.27%. What's the difference between benchmarking and an RFP?

Benchmarking compares your current plan to industry standards using available data. An RFP (Request for Proposal) is a formal process where competing providers submit actual bids to service your plan. Our Live Bid benchmarking combines both approaches, using real RFP responses rather than industry averages for maximum accuracy. This methodology reveals your true competitive position and creates negotiating leverage with your current provider or alternatives.

Is 401(k) benchmarking required by law?

ERISA requires plan fiduciaries to ensure fees are reasonable but doesn't mandate formal benchmarking. However, proving you fulfilled your fiduciary responsibility requires documentation. The DOL suggests benchmarking every 3-5 years, and failure to benchmark is often cited in excessive fee lawsuits. With ERISA litigation up 35% in 2024 and average settlements at $4.6 million, regular benchmarking provides essential liability protection through documented due diligence.
