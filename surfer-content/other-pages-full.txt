Who We Serve
You wear a lot of hats. Running your business, managing your team, and somewhere in between, making sure your company's retirement plan stays compliant, competitive, and genuinely helpful to the people who depend on it. That's a tall order, especially when the regulatory landscape keeps shifting and the stakes keep rising.
PointOak Retirement Advisors works exclusively with plan sponsors and plan fiduciaries who want more than a checkbox advisor. We serve organizations across the United States that are ready for a true retirement plan specialist, one that brings independent advice, fiduciary accountability, and a process built to protect you.

Business Owners and C-Suite Executives
You built this company. The retirement plan should reflect that same commitment to your workforce, not become another source of liability. But between ERISA obligations, DOL oversight, and the constant pressure to balance costs against employee outcomes, it's easy for your 401(k) plan to fall into the "we'll deal with it later" category.
That's where we come in. PointOak helps business owners and executives take control of their retirement plan strategy without it consuming their calendar. We act as your 3(21) or 3(38) named fiduciary under ERISA, which means we share or assume the fiduciary responsibility that would otherwise rest on your shoulders.
You focus on growing your business. We focus on making sure your plan is legally sound, cost-effective, and working for your employees.
What matters most to you:
* Minimizing personal fiduciary liability exposure
* Ensuring plan fees are competitive without sacrificing participant outcomes
* Offering a retirement benefit that attracts and retains top talent
* Having documented, defensible processes in case of a DOL audit

CFOs and Financial Officers

Plan Fiduciaries and Investment Committee Members
Whether you volunteered or were volunteered, serving on your company's Investment Committee comes with real legal responsibility. ERISA's fiduciary standards of care require prudent decision-making, proper documentation, and ongoing monitoring of plan investments and service providers. That's a significant obligation for anyone whose primary job isn't managing retirement plans.
PointOak's investment analysis services give committee members the tools, data, and expert guidance to fulfill their fiduciary duties with confidence. We help identify or establish your Investment Committee structure, draft your Investment Policy Statement, and conduct quarterly reviews using institutional measurement techniques that evaluate every fund in your lineup against objective criteria.
Our Virtual 401(k) Fiduciary File documents every decision, every meeting, and every recommendation, creating a clear paper trail that protects committee members if the plan is ever audited. We've been registered and acting as fiduciaries since day one. This isn't a service we added to our menu; it's the foundation of our practice.
What matters most to you:
* Understanding and fulfilling personal fiduciary obligations
* Having expert support for investment selection and monitoring
* Maintaining comprehensive documentation for audit protection
* Accessing independent advice free from conflicts of interest

Frequently Asked Questions
What types of retirement plans does PointOak support?
We work with plan sponsors who offer 401(k) plans, 403(b) plans, defined benefit plans, and other employer-sponsored retirement plans. Our consulting services are structured to meet the unique requirements of each plan type and the fiduciary obligations that come with them.
Do I need to be local to work with PointOak?
Not at all. While our office is based in McLean, Virginia, we serve plan sponsors across the entire United States. Most of our consulting, benchmarking, and investment analysis work is delivered through a combination of virtual meetings and on-site visits as needed.
How is PointOak different from our current financial advisor?
Most financial advisors are generalists who manage retirement plans as one of many product lines. PointOak is a retirement plan specialist, generating over 90% of our revenue from corporate retirement plan consulting. That singular focus gives us deeper expertise, more current regulatory knowledge, and a consulting process purpose-built for plan sponsors and fiduciaries.
What does it mean that PointOak acts as a named fiduciary?
As a named fiduciary under ERISA, PointOak takes on direct legal responsibility for the advice and recommendations we provide. Under a 3(21) arrangement, we serve as a co-fiduciary and provide guidance while you retain decision-making authority. Under a 3(38) arrangement, we assume discretionary responsibility for selecting and monitoring plan investments.
Either way, we share the fiduciary burden with you rather than leaving it entirely on your plate.
How do I know if my current plan needs a specialist?
If you're unsure whether your plan fees are competitive, haven't benchmarked your provider in three or more years, struggle with low participation rates, or simply feel overwhelmed by ERISA compliance, those are strong signals that a specialist can add immediate value. Contact us for a no-obligation conversation about where your plan stands.

Analyze. Optimize. Implement.
Our process isn't a one-size-fits-all checklist. It's a meticulous, technology-driven methodology developed over years of working exclusively with plan sponsors like you. We start by understanding where your plan stands today, identify what needs to change, and then make it happen.
Because we generate over 90% of our revenue from working with corporate retirement plans, this process has been refined by real-world experience across a wide range of engagements. You get the benefit of all that insight applied directly to your plan.
________________


Phase 1: Analyze
We Start by Understanding Your Plan Inside and Out
Before we recommend a single change, we need to know exactly what you're working with. Our analysis phase is comprehensive, covering your plan's structure, fees, investments, provider relationships, and fiduciary processes.
Here's what we evaluate during this phase:
Plan Design Review: We assess your current plan structure, including enrollment features, contribution formulas, vesting schedules, and eligibility provisions. Are your plan's design elements aligned with your company's goals and culture? We'll find out.
Fee and Provider Benchmarking: You've certainly thought about cost. But how much is too much, and what are you getting for your money? We conduct a custom Live Bid benchmarking study that reviews over 450 data points per bidder, using your actual plan information rather than average demographics.
Investment Lineup Evaluation: With more than 40,000 funds available, selecting the right investment menu is no small task. Our investment analysis methodology measures fund performance across multiple factors, including fund style, risk-adjusted returns, peer group rankings, manager tenure, and expense ratios.
Fiduciary Process Assessment: Are your processes documented? Is your Investment Committee structured properly? Do you have a current Investment Policy Statement? We identify gaps in your fiduciary compliance and create a clear roadmap for closing them.
Participant Outcomes Review: We look at participation rates, deferral levels, and retirement readiness metrics to understand how well your plan is actually serving your employees.
The result of this phase is a clear, detailed picture of where your plan stands and where the opportunities are. No guesswork. No assumptions. Just data.
________________


Phase 2: Optimize
We Build a Strategy Tailored to Your Plan
Once we understand your plan's current state, we develop specific, actionable recommendations designed to strengthen every aspect of your retirement program. This isn't about making changes for the sake of change. It's about making the right changes for your organization and your employees.
Investment Menu Optimization: Based on our analysis, we recommend adjustments to your investment lineup to reduce costs, improve diversification, and align fund options with your participants' needs. Every recommendation is backed by our quarterly monitoring data and documented in your Investment Policy Statement.
Fee Negotiation and Provider Strategy: We help you uncover inefficiencies and then provide you with the data to renegotiate better fees and services, or to make a provider switch. Our benchmarking data gives you real leverage because it's based on live bids from competing providers, not industry averages.
Plan Design Enhancements: We help you move beyond a cookie-cutter plan. Whether it's implementing automatic enrollment, adding auto-escalation features, or restructuring your match formula, we tailor design recommendations to your specific company goals and workforce demographics.
Fiduciary Framework Development: We draft or update your Investment Policy Statement, establish or strengthen your Investment Committee structure, and build the governance framework that protects you. Acting as your 3(21) or 3(38) named fiduciary under ERISA, we share the fiduciary responsibility so you're not carrying it alone.
Target Date Fund Evaluation: If your plan uses TDFs (and most do), we conduct a multi-step suitability analysis to determine whether your current solution is the right fit for your participants, or whether a different approach would serve them better.
Every recommendation comes with clear documentation of our rationale, making your decisions defensible if you're ever audited.
________________


Phase 3: Implement
We Put the Plan Into Action and Keep It on Track
Recommendations only matter if they're executed well. We work directly with your providers, recordkeepers, and third-party administrators to implement every change efficiently and accurately.
Implementation isn't the finish line. It's the starting point for ongoing monitoring and support.
Change Execution: We coordinate with all parties to implement investment changes, plan design updates, provider transitions, and fee renegotiations. You don't have to manage the logistics.
Quarterly Investment Monitoring: Your plan doesn't stand still, and neither do we. We monitor your investment lineup every quarter using institutional-grade analytics, flagging any funds that fall below our performance thresholds and providing specific recommendations when action is needed.
Annual Fiduciary Plan Review: Each year, we deliver a comprehensive review presentation covering your plan's performance, compliance status, fee competitiveness, and participant outcomes. This keeps you informed and ensures your fiduciary file stays current.
Regulatory Updates and Education: The DOL and ERISA landscape changes constantly. We stay on top of evolving fiduciary rules and regulations so we can implement current compliance practices on your behalf. You'll never be caught off guard by a new requirement.
Employee Education Programs: A strong plan means nothing if employees don't use it. Our licensed securities advisors lead interactive sessions covering retirement savings strategies, budgeting, and debt management to help your workforce take full advantage of the plan you've built.
Virtual 401(k) Fiduciary File: We document everything we do with you in a secure digital file. Every analysis, every recommendation, every committee meeting, every decision. If you're ever audited, your documentation is already in order.

Named Fiduciary Protection

PointOak's advisors are registered and act as fiduciaries under ERISA, serving as either a 3(21) co-fiduciary or 3(38) investment manager with all clients. We've been doing this since the first day we started our practice.
