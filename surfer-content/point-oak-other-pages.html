How We Work: Analyze. Optimize. Implement.

We follow a three-phase approach that brings structure and accountability to every engagement.

Analyze. We start by examining your current plan structure, fees, investments, and processes to identify where improvements will have the greatest impact. This isn't a surface-level review. We dig into the details so nothing gets overlooked.

Optimize. Based on our findings, we develop strategic recommendations on plan design, investments, and provider relationships. Every recommendation is tailored to your organization's goals, your participants' needs, and the regulatory landscape you're operating in.

Implement. We put the plan into action and provide ongoing monitoring to make sure your retirement plan keeps performing. Quarterly investment reviews, annual benchmarking studies, and regulatory updates keep you ahead of changes rather than reacting to them.

Every step is documented meticulously. If you ever face an audit, your Virtual 401(k) Fiduciary File contains a complete record of every process, procedure, and decision, built to demonstrate the prudent process the DOL expects.

Do I need to be local to work with PointOak?

How is PointOak different from our current financial advisor?

PointOak is a retirement plan specialist, generating over 90% of our revenue from corporate retirement plan consulting. What does it mean that PointOak acts as a named fiduciary?

As a named fiduciary under ERISA, PointOak takes on direct legal responsibility for the advice and recommendations we provide. Under a 3(21) arrangement, we serve as a co-fiduciary and provide guidance while you retain decision-making authority. Under a 3(38) arrangement, we assume discretionary responsibility for selecting and monitoring plan investments. Either way, we share the fiduciary burden with you rather than leaving it entirely on your plate.

How do I know if my current plan needs a specialist?

If you're unsure whether your plan fees are competitive, haven't benchmarked your provider in three or more years, struggle with low participation rates, or simply feel overwhelmed by ERISA compliance, those are strong signals that a specialist can add immediate value. Contact us for a no-obligation conversation about where your plan stands.

Phase 1: Analyze

We Start by Understanding Your Plan Inside and Out

Before we recommend a single change, we need to know exactly what you're working with. Our analysis phase is comprehensive, covering your plan's structure, fees, investments, provider relationships, and fiduciary processes.

Here's what we evaluate during this phase:

  • Plan Design Review: We assess your current plan structure, including enrollment features, contribution formulas, vesting schedules, and eligibility provisions. Are your plan's design elements aligned with your company's goals and culture? We'll find out.
  • Fee and Provider Benchmarking: You've certainly thought about cost. But how much is too much, and what are you getting for your money? We conduct a custom Live Bid benchmarking study that reviews over 450 data points per bidder, using your actual plan information rather than average demographics. This makes our analysis among the most accurate in the industry.
  • Investment Lineup Evaluation: With more than 40,000 funds available, selecting the right investment menu is no small task. Our investment analysis methodology evaluates over $120 billion in retirement plan assets quarterly, measuring fund performance across multiple factors using our proprietary 10-point pass/fail scoring system.
  • Fiduciary Process Assessment: Are your processes documented? Is your Investment Committee structured properly? Do you have a current Investment Policy Statement? We identify gaps in your fiduciary compliance and create a clear roadmap for closing them.
  • Participant Outcomes Review: We look at participation rates, deferral levels, and retirement readiness metrics to understand how well your plan is actually serving your employees.

The result of this phase is a clear, detailed picture of where your plan stands and where the opportunities are. No guesswork. No assumptions. Just data.

Phase 2: Optimize

We Build a Strategy Tailored to Your Plan

Once we understand your plan's current state, we develop specific, actionable recommendations designed to strengthen every aspect of your retirement program.

This isn't about making changes for the sake of change. It's about making the right changes for your organization and your employees.

  • Investment Menu Optimization: Based on our analysis, we recommend adjustments to your investment lineup to reduce costs, improve diversification, and align fund options with your participants' needs. Every recommendation is backed by our quarterly monitoring data and documented in your Investment Policy Statement.
  • Fee Negotiation and Provider Strategy: We help you uncover inefficiencies. And then we provide you with the ammunition to renegotiate better fees and better services, or to make a provider switch. Our benchmarking data gives you real leverage because it's based on live bids from competing providers, not industry averages.
  • Plan Design Enhancements: We help you move beyond a cookie-cutter plan. Whether it's implementing automatic enrollment, adding auto-escalation features, or restructuring your match formula, we tailor design recommendations to your specific company goals and workforce demographics.
  • Fiduciary Framework Development: We draft or update your Investment Policy Statement, establish or strengthen your Investment Committee structure, and build the governance framework that protects you. Acting as your 3(21) or 3(38) named fiduciary under ERISA, we share the fiduciary responsibility so you're not carrying it alone.
  • Target Date Fund Evaluation: If your plan uses TDFs (and most do), we conduct a multi-step suitability analysis to determine whether your current solution is the right fit for your participants, or whether a different approach would serve them better.

Every recommendation comes with clear documentation of our rationale, making your decisions defensible if you're ever audited.

Phase 3: Implement

We Put the Plan Into Action and Keep It on Track

Recommendations only matter if they're executed well. We work directly with your providers, recordkeepers, and third-party administrators to implement every change efficiently and accurately.

But implementation isn't the finish line. It's the starting point for ongoing monitoring and support.

  • Change Execution: We coordinate with all parties to implement investment changes, plan design updates, provider transitions, and fee renegotiations. You don't have to manage the logistics.
  • Quarterly Investment Monitoring: Your plan doesn't stand still, and neither do we. We monitor your investment lineup every quarter using institutional-grade analytics, flagging any funds that fall below our performance thresholds and providing specific recommendations when action is needed.
  • Annual Fiduciary Plan Review: Each year, we deliver a comprehensive review presentation covering your plan's performance, compliance status, fee competitiveness, and participant outcomes. This keeps you informed and ensures your fiduciary file stays current.
  • Regulatory Updates and Education: The DOL and ERISA landscape changes constantly. We stay on top of the ever-changing fiduciary rules and regulations so we can implement best practices to keep you in compliance. You'll never be caught off guard by a new requirement.
  • Employee Education Programs: A great plan means nothing if employees don't use it. Our licensed securities advisors lead interactive sessions covering retirement savings strategies, budgeting, and debt management to help your workforce take full advantage of the plan you've built.
  • Virtual 401(k) Fiduciary File: We document everything we do with you in a secure digital file. Every analysis, every recommendation, every committee meeting, every decision. If you're ever audited, your documentation is already in order.

Fiduciary Services

What is a fiduciary, and why does it matter for my retirement plan?

A fiduciary is someone who is legally obligated to act in the best interests of your plan's participants. Under ERISA, plan sponsors have fiduciary duties that carry personal liability. If you're not meeting those obligations, you and your organization could face penalties, lawsuits, or DOL enforcement actions.

That's where we come in. PointOak's advisors are registered and act as named fiduciaries under ERISA, helping to protect you from liability while making sure your plan stays compliant. You can explore our full approach on our Fiduciary Support page.

What is the difference between a 3(21) and 3(38) fiduciary?

These are two distinct fiduciary roles defined under ERISA:

  • 3(21) Co-Fiduciary: We provide recommendations and guidance on investment decisions, but you (the plan sponsor) retain final decision-making authority.
  • 3(38) Investment Manager: We take on discretionary authority for selecting and monitoring your plan's investments, assuming direct responsibility for those decisions.

Both roles provide meaningful liability protection. The right fit depends on how much involvement you want in the investment decision-making process. We work with you to determine which structure best suits your plan's needs.

What is a Virtual 401(k) Fiduciary File?

It's a digital documentation system we maintain for every client. We document all processes, procedures, meetings, and decisions related to your retirement plan. If you're ever audited by the DOL, this file serves as your paper trail, showing that you followed a prudent, well-documented process in managing your plan.

What is an Investment Policy Statement (IPS)?

An IPS is a written document that outlines your plan's investment objectives, guidelines, and the criteria used to select and monitor investments. Think of it as the rulebook for your plan's investment decisions. It helps your Investment Committee stay disciplined and provides documentation that supports your fiduciary obligations.

We help draft and maintain your IPS as part of our investment analysis services.

What are target date funds, and how do you evaluate them?

Target date funds (TDFs) are investment options that automatically adjust their asset allocation as participants approach retirement age. They've become the most common default investment option in retirement plans, but not all TDFs are built the same.

Our TDF consulting process includes a multi-step suitability and selection analysis. We evaluate participant demographics, compare glide path strategies ("to" retirement vs. "through" retirement), review fund fees and share classes, and assess historical performance. The goal is to make sure your TDF selection is truly aligned with your participants' characteristics. Learn more on our Target Date Fund Consulting page.

Fees and Benchmarking

How often should we benchmark our retirement plan?

The DOL requires that plans be benchmarked at least every three years to determine reasonableness of fees. However, we recommend annual benchmarking to stay ahead of market changes and maintain leverage with your current provider.

What is PointOak's benchmarking process?

We conduct custom "Live Bid" benchmarking studies that use your actual plan information rather than average plan demographics. Each study reviews over 450 data points per bidder across three key areas: fees, investments, and services. This approach gives you a far more accurate picture of how your plan stacks up than standard industry benchmarks.

The results give you the ammunition to renegotiate better fees or services with your current provider, or to make a well-informed provider switch if needed. Visit our Provider & Fee Benchmarking page for details.

How do I know if my plan's fees are reasonable?

That's a question more plan sponsors should be asking. The truth is, it's hard to know without a proper benchmark. Fees can be buried across multiple layers (recordkeeper fees, investment expenses, advisory fees, and more), and what looks competitive on the surface may not hold up under scrutiny.

We help you uncover inefficiencies and compare your total plan costs against similar-sized plans in your market. Our goal is that you never pay more than you should.
