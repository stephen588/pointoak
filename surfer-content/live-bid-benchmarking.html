What's the difference between 3(21) and 3(38) fiduciary services?

A 3(21) co-fiduciary provides recommendations and advice on retirement plan costs, investments, and providers, but you retain final decision-making authority. A 3(38) investment manager assumes discretionary responsibility for selecting and monitoring plan investments, reducing your fiduciary liability. Both roles require us to act in your best interest and document our process as ERISA requires. The right choice depends on your comfort level with investment decisions and how much liability protection you want. We help you evaluate which structure makes sense for your situation and ensures services delivered meet your service expectations.
