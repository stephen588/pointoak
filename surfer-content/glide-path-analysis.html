How long does a target date fund glide path evaluation usually take?

A comprehensive glide path evaluation typically takes four to six weeks from receiving plan data to delivering recommendations. Complex evaluations involving multiple provider comparisons may require additional time. We work with your timeline for urgent situations requiring faster turnaround.

What is the difference between a "to" and "through" retirement glide path?

A "to retirement" glide path reaches its most conservative portfolio at the target retirement date and maintains that asset mix afterward. A "through retirement" approach continues shifting toward more conservative investments for years past the target date. The right approach depends on your participant population characteristics and retirement planning patterns.

How do I know if my target date fund glide path is appropriate for plan participants?

Appropriate glide paths align with your participant population's characteristics. Key factors include average retirement date, salary levels, contribution rates, and whether participants have other retirement savings sources. We evaluate your workforce data against your current TDF's assumptions to identify mismatches that could affect retirement outcomes.

Can target date funds lose money at or after the target retirement date?

Yes. Target date funds are not guaranteed, and past performance is not necessarily indicative of future results. Plan participants can lose money at any time, including at and after the target date. Funds with higher risk through more equity exposure at retirement carry more investment risk but also more growth potential.

What key factors should plan sponsors consider when evaluating glide paths?

DOL guidance emphasizes several factors: the fund's underlying funds and how asset allocation changes over time, when the fund reaches its most conservative point, fees and expenses, alignment with your participant population, and whether the fund uses a "to" or "through" retirement approach for investing through the retirement years.

How often should target date fund selections undergo periodic review?

DOL guidance recommends periodic review of all investments including target date funds. At minimum, review when there are significant changes to the fund's investment strategies, management team, or your participant population. Most plan sponsors conduct annual reviews as part of overall Investment Analysis.

Is a conservative glide path always better for participants near retirement?

Not necessarily. Participants who work past the approximate year of their target date have extended accumulation periods and may benefit from more equity exposure to manage inflation risk. A conservative portfolio designed for retirement at 65 may be too conservative for someone with individual circumstances that include working until 70. This requires careful analysis of your specific participant population.

Should plan participants get individual investment advice about target date funds?

Target date funds are designed as all-in-one solutions for retirement planning, but some participants may benefit from working with a financial professional for more personalized allocations based on their individual circumstances and financial situation. We help plan sponsors provide further information and education so participants can make informed decisions. PointOak does not provide tax advice or legal or tax advice to individual participants.
