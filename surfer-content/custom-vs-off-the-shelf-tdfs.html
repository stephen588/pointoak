Glide Path Comparison

The glide path determines how asset allocation changes over time as participants age, making glide path design the most important element in any target date strategy. We conduct side-by-side comparisons of glide paths from multiple providers. Plans with mostly younger workers expect different asset allocation patterns than those with employees nearing retirement.

We analyze whether a "to retirement" approach (reaching maximum conservatism at the target date) or "through retirement" design (continuing to adjust past retirement) better serves your plan participants. The comparison includes modeling potential outcomes under different market scenarios to illustrate how each glide path would perform.

What is the difference between custom and off-the-shelf target date funds?

Off-the-shelf target date funds use a standardized glide path designed for the average investor, with asset allocation based solely on expected retirement year. Custom target date funds tailor the glide path to your client's specific plan demographics, allowing more control over underlying asset classes selection, manager choices, and how investments gradually shift from growth to conservative allocations. Plans with concentrated age groups or unique characteristics often achieve better retirement outcomes through customization aligned with their population's actual needs throughout their working life.

What participant demographics should trigger consideration of custom TDFs?

Several factors suggest custom solutions may better serve your plan based on sources believed reliable through industry research: participant ages concentrated in narrow ranges rather than evenly distributed, average account balances significantly above or below national medians, high percentage of highly compensated employees, existing defined benefit plan coverage affecting retirement income needs, or unique risk tolerance patterns. DOL guidance emphasizes evaluating these characteristics across a broad range of factors when selecting target date funds to ensure alignment with your workforce reality.

How does DOL guidance apply to custom target date fund selection?

The Department of Labor's 2013 tips for ERISA plan fiduciaries require evaluating whether a target date fund's investment strategies and characteristics align with your plan's participant demographics. This suitability analysis should consider participant age distribution, retirement income sources, risk capacity, and financial sophistication across the participant population. Plan sponsors must document the selection process and monitor performance over time. Proper documentation demonstrates the prudent, research-based process DOL expects during plan audits and protects fiduciaries from liability in an increasingly complex regulatory world.

Can custom target date funds lower expenses compared to off-the-shelf?

Yes, in many cases. While off-the-shelf target date funds lock you into a single fund family's investment options and other funds within that ecosystem, custom solutions allow selecting lower-cost managers where passive exposure suffices while using active management only where research shows it historically adds value. Collective investment trust structures often carry lower expense ratios than comparable mutual funds. The key is strategic manager selection giving clients more control rather than blanket active or passive approaches, which can reduce costs while maintaining diversification across economic cycles.

What are the benefits of custom target date funds?

Custom target date funds offer several advantages: glide paths tailored to your actual participant demographics rather than generic assumptions intended for the average investor, flexibility to incorporate best-in-class managers across underlying asset classes instead of being limited to one fund family, potential for lower investment expenses through strategic active/passive blending, ability to adjust asset allocation as your workforce evolves, and comprehensive fiduciary documentation demonstrating DOL-compliant suitability analysis. Custom solutions provide more control over how the retirement strategy serves participants throughout their working life.

Custom solutions can maintain higher equity allocations appropriate for this group while still providing diversification and professional management. Research shows that participant-specific design aligned with client's specific workforce characteristics improves retirement outcomes compared to other funds using generic approaches.
