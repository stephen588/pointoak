How long does the TDF selection process usually take?

A comprehensive target date fund evaluation typically takes 4-6 weeks from initial analysis through final documentation. This timeline includes reviewing participant demographics, comparing glide paths across fund families, evaluating fees and performance, and preparing detailed reports. Implementation timing depends on your recordkeeper's processes and any participant communication requirements.

What is the difference between "to" and "through" retirement glide paths?

A "to retirement" glide path reaches its most conservative asset allocation at the target date, assuming participants will withdraw all savings when they retire. A "through retirement" glide path continues reducing equity exposure for 10-20 years past the target date, maintaining some growth potential for participants who draw down gradually. The right choice depends on your participant characteristics and withdrawal tendencies.

How do I choose the right target date fund for my 401(k) plan?

Start by analyzing your participant demographics including age distribution, salary levels, and expected retirement patterns. Then evaluate which glide path philosophy aligns with those characteristics. Compare fees to ensure you're getting appropriate share classes for your plan size. Finally, assess underlying fund quality and manager tenure. Document each step of this evaluation process for fiduciary protection.

What are the risks of target date funds?

Target date funds carry several important risks plan sponsors must understand. The principal value is not guaranteed at any time, including at the target date. Participants can lose money if the stocks and bonds owned by the fund decline. Different TDFs with the same target date may have vastly different risk levels due to glide path variations. Plus, participants who don't understand the fund's assumptions may end up with unsuitable allocations.

What did the DOL say about target date funds?

The DOL published "Target Date Retirement Funds: Tips for ERISA Plan Fiduciaries" in 2013. This guidance makes clear that plan fiduciaries must establish a process for comparing and selecting target date funds, considering factors like the fund's glide path, the fund's investment objectives and strategies, and how well the TDF aligns with participant demographics. Simply picking a TDF based on target date alone doesn't meet fiduciary standards.

How often should target date funds be reviewed?

The DOL expects ongoing monitoring, not just evaluation at selection. We recommend quarterly reviews to track performance, verify the glide path remains appropriate, confirm fees haven't increased, and ensure no significant changes in fund managers or investment strategies. This regular monitoring should be documented in your fiduciary file to demonstrate continuous oversight.

Our plan has 3 different TDF families with the same target dates but different allocations. How do we choose?

This is exactly why the DOL requires documented evaluation. Compare each family's glide path against your participant demographics and retirement tendencies. Evaluate the quality of underlying funds in each series. Benchmark fees across all three options. Consider which glide path philosophy aligns with your employees' likely withdrawal patterns. Document which factors you weighted most heavily and why. The goal isn't picking the "best" TDF universally, but the best fit for your specific plan.
