What documentation is required for target date fund selection?

The DOL expects documentation showing you established an objective process for comparing and selecting TDFs, understood the fund's investments and asset allocation glide path, reviewed the fund's fees and expenses, considered whether custom options includes component investments not managed by your TDF vendor would better fit your plan, developed effective participant communications, and maintained records of how you reached decisions on individual investment options. We help ensure all elements are properly documented.

How often should plan fiduciaries review target date funds?

Plan fiduciaries should monitor funds at minimum on an annual basis, with many experts recommending quarterly monitoring for performance tracking. Reviews should examine whether there have been significant changes in any criteria you considered when selecting TDFs, including investment strategy changes, management staff turnover, fee modifications, or shifts in participant demographics. We provide quarterly monitoring support to help you stay current.

What is the difference between "to" and "through" TDF glide paths?

A "to retirement" glide path reaches its most conservative investments at the target retirement date, while a "through retirement" fixed date approaches continue shifting after the approximate date. As the fund nears retirement years, asset allocation becomes more conservative to reduce volatility. Neither approach is inherently better; appropriate choice depends on your plan participants' characteristics. Documenting why your selected glide path suits your workforce is essential.

Can plan sponsors be sued for target date fund performance?

Yes. Recent litigation has expanded beyond fee-only claims to challenge TDF performance directly. The $69 million UnitedHealth settlement involved allegations of selecting retirement funds that underperformed 70-90% of peers. Plaintiffs' attorneys are actively investigating TDF underperformance across the industry. The automatic rebalancing feature doesn't protect fiduciaries from scrutiny. Proper selection documentation is your primary defense against such claims.

What should be included in a TDF fiduciary file?

Your TDF fiduciary file should contain your Investment Policy Statement, documentation of the objective process including comparison criteria and evaluation results, Investment Committee meeting minutes, fee analysis covering expenses charged and benchmarking reports, participant demographic data supporting suitability, ongoing monitoring records showing how you monitor funds, and any communications or education materials. We help organize and maintain all required documentation on an annual basis.

How do we evaluate whether our recordkeeper's proprietary TDF is the best option?

DOL guidance explicitly states ERISA plan fiduciaries should inquire about custom or non-proprietary alternatives, including whether component funds from other managers might better serve your plan. Things DOL suggests include evaluating your TDF vendor's funds against independent options using the same criteria: performance, the fund's fees, glide path suitability, and quality of component investments. Document your comparison using commercially available resources regardless of outcome.

What benchmarks should we use to compare target date fund performance?

TDF benchmarking should include peer group comparisons for funds with similar target retirement date vintages, risk-adjusted return metrics over a longer period, and underlying manager evaluation. No single benchmark tells the complete story. We evaluate TDFs from multiple perspectives including performance against peers, adherence to stated primary strategy, and alignment with your participants savings needs, documenting findings for your fiduciary records.
