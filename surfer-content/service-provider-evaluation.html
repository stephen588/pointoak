Hidden costs and fee opacity

Many 401(k) providers charge indirect costs through revenue sharing or investment expenses that don't appear on invoices, making transparent fees comparison across different providers nearly impossible.

Our Live Bid process analyzes all fee sources including revenue sharing to create a transparent fee structure comparison across 450+ data points per potential vendor.

Employers can't objectively measure service quality like nondiscrimination testing accuracy, response times, or plan setup capabilities without technical expertise to identify red flags.

Should I use a 3(21) or 3(38) fiduciary for provider evaluation?

Both fiduciary types can help with the evaluation process, but they differ in scope of fiduciary responsibilities. A 3(21) co-fiduciary provides recommendations and guidance while you retain final decision authority. A 3(38) investment manager assumes discretionary responsibility for investment selection and ongoing monitoring. The choice depends on your internal resources and desired level of involvement. We offer both options and help you determine which fits your specific needs.
