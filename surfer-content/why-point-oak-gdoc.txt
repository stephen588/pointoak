Named Fiduciary Protection
One of the most important questions you can ask a retirement plan advisor is this: will you put your name on it?
PointOak's advisors are registered and act as fiduciaries under ERISA, serving as either a 3(21) co-fiduciary or 3(38) investment manager with all clients. We've been doing this since the first day we started our practice. A 3(21) fiduciary provides recommendations and guidance while you retain final decision-making authority. A 3(38) fiduciary assumes discretionary responsibility for selecting and monitoring your plan's investments.
Either way, we share the fiduciary burden with you. That's not just a talking point. It's a legal commitment that directly reduces your liability exposure and demonstrates that your plan is receiving guidance from a named fiduciary who is legally bound to act in your participants' best interests.

A Benchmarking Process Built for Accuracy
You've certainly thought about cost. But how much is too much, and what are you getting for your money? How does your plan stack up against other similar-sized plans in your market?
These are prudent questions, but the research required can be a drain on your time. PointOak performs over 10,000 benchmarks per year through our comprehensive "Live Bid" benchmarking process. Unlike standard industry benchmarks that rely on average plan demographics, our process uses your specific plan information to deliver results that reflect your actual situation.
We review over 450 data points per bidder across three critical areas: fees, investments, and services. That level of detail helps you uncover inefficiencies and gives you the ammunition to renegotiate better terms with your current provider, or to make a switch if the data supports it.

Investment Expertise at Scale
With over 40,000 mutual funds available, building and maintaining the right investment menu for your plan is no small task. PointOak's investment analysis methodology evaluates over $120 billion in retirement plan assets every quarter. We measure fund performance across multiple factors, including fund style, risk-adjusted returns, peer group rankings, fund manager tenure, and expense ratios.
Our proprietary 10-point pass/fail scoring system combines sophisticated institutional measurement techniques with analytics that are clear enough for your Investment Committee to act on with confidence. We don't just hand you a report full of jargon. We give you specific, documented recommendations on which funds to keep, which to watch, and which to replace.
That's where our investment process and methodology benefits you as the plan sponsor. You get the rigor of an institutional evaluation with the clarity of an advisor who knows how to communicate what the numbers mean for your employees' retirement readiness.

Process-Driven Protection
If the DOL comes knocking, the first thing they'll ask for is documentation. Can you show that you followed a prudent process for selecting investments? Can you demonstrate that fees were reviewed and benchmarked? Can you prove that fiduciary decisions were made with appropriate oversight?
PointOak applies a meticulous, technology-driven process developed over years of client engagement. We document everything we do with you, from investment reviews and committee meetings to benchmarking studies and plan design changes. All of this is maintained in our Virtual 401(k) Fiduciary File, a digital record that helps protect you from liability if you're ever audited.
Our approach follows three phases: Analyze your current plan structure, fees, investments, and processes. Optimize through strategic recommendations tailored to your goals. Implement changes and provide ongoing monitoring to ensure continued success.
